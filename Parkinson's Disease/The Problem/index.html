What is Parkinson's Disease?

Before we talk about anything else, we need to learn the basics of Parkinson's Disease. What even is it? Parkison's Disease is considered a progressive neurodegenerative disease.

What this basically means is that the condition of the patient worsens over time. Those who have Parkinson's Disease lose neurons in the brain over time. These neurons are a spefic type of neurons called Dopamine Neurons.

The substantia-nigra, found in your brain

Dopamine

Dopamine neurons are a specific type of neurons in your brain. Dopamine neurons can be found in the region of your brain known as the substantia nigra. These dopamine neurons are responsible for creating a chemical called dopamine. Dopamine is typically known for being responsible for happiness and motivation. However, dopamine is also responsible for movement.

Parkinson's Disease causes these dopamine neurons to die. This in turn causes there to be less dopamine in your brain. The downward spike in dopamine causes the main movement problems that are seen as symptoms in Parkinson's Disease.

Risk Factors

There a certain things, risk factors, that can make a patient more vulnerable to Parkinson's Disease. The risk factors for Parkinson's Disease are:

  • Age
  • Gender
  • Genetics
  • Enviornmental Causes
  • Head Injury
  • Medications/Drugs

Age

Compared to all the other risk factors, age is the biggest one. Around 1%-2% of all people above 60 years old gets Parkinson's Disease at some point.
